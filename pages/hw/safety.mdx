# Being Safe with a Humanoid Robot

<Callout type="warning" emoji="⚠️">
Be very careful! In order to be useful, robots need to be relatively strong, but this strength means that it is capable of causing serious injury if used improperly. You should treat your hardware with respect and make sure to follow common sense safety practices whenever you are working with it.
</Callout>

## General Safety Tips

1. **Read the Manual**: Always read the robot's user manual thoroughly before operation.
2. **Training**: Ensure all operators are properly trained and understand the robot's capabilities and limitations.
3. **Emergency Procedures**: Familiarize yourself with emergency stop procedures and ensure they are easily accessible.

## Physical Safety

1. **Safe Distance**: Maintain a safe distance from the robot when it is in operation.
2. **Protective Gear**: Wear appropriate protective gear, such as gloves and safety glasses, when necessary.
3. **Secure Environment**: Ensure the robot is operated in a secure environment, free from obstacles and hazards.

## Operational Safety

1. **Power Off**: Always power off the robot when not in use or when performing maintenance.
2. **Regular Maintenance**: Conduct regular maintenance checks to ensure all components are functioning correctly.
3. **Load Limits**: Do not exceed the robot's specified load limits to prevent mechanical failure.

## Electrical Safety

1. **Proper Wiring**: Ensure all electrical connections are secure and insulated.
2. **Emergency Shut-off**: When connecting the robot directly to a power supply, it is important to make sure the power supply can be quickly and reliably powered off.

## Software Safety

1. **Updates**: Regularly update the robot's software to patch vulnerabilities and improve performance.
2. **Testing**: Test new software in a controlled environment before deploying it on the robot.
3. **Access Control**: Implement access controls to prevent unauthorized use of the robot.

## Interaction Safety

1. **Clear Communication**: Use clear and consistent communication signals when interacting with the robot.
2. **Avoid Distractions**: Stay focused and avoid distractions when operating or interacting with the robot.
3. **Supervision**: Always supervise the robot when it is in operation, especially in public or crowded areas.

## Emergency Preparedness

1. **First Aid**: Have a first aid kit readily available and ensure personnel are trained in basic first aid.
2. **Fire Safety**: Ensure fire extinguishers are available and personnel are trained in their use.
