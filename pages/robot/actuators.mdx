## ID Layout

We use the following numbering scheme for the actuators in our robot:

The numbering scheme follows a **logical** top-down, left-to-right approach that makes it **easy to identify and locate actuators:**

- **11-19**: Left arm (11-16), starting from shoulder to hand
- **21-29**: Right arm (21-26), mirroring the left arm
- **31-39**: Left leg (31-35), from hip to ankle
- **41-49**: Right leg (41-45), mirroring the left leg

This **systematic approach** helps technicians and programmers **quickly identify** actuator locations during maintenance and programming. The tens digit indicates the limb segment, while the ones digit represents the specific joint within that segment.

By allocating 9 IDs for each limb, this scheme **provides flexibility** to accommodate different robotic embodiments. This allows for easy adaptation and scalability, ensuring that the numbering system can be consistently applied across various robot designs and configurations.
